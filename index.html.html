The 8‑Step Engagement Plan

  1. 1) Kickoff & Discovery

    Book a no‑obligation consult to define goals, scope, and KPIs (stock accuracy, cycle‑count time, loss prevention, asset utilisation).

  2. 2) Site Survey & Feasibility

    On‑site assessment of layout, materials (metal/liquids), read zones, antenna placements, tag shortlists, and expected read rates.

  3. 3) Compliance Planning (Malaysia)

    Align readers/tags to the Malaysia UHF RFID band (919–923 MHz). Confirm device approvals and follow current MCMC Class Assignment conditions.

  4. 4) Solution Design

    Define architecture: reader mix, antenna maps, tag strategy, middleware, cloud platform, and integrations (ERP/WMS/POS) with acceptance criteria.

  5. 5) Pilot / Proof‑of‑Concept

    Run a low‑risk pilot at 1–2 sites/zones to validate read rates and process fit. Measure accuracy, cycle‑count time, and locate‑time improvements.

  6. 6) Deployment & Integration

    Procure & install certified hardware; configure dashboards, alerts, and API links to your business systems; cut over with a staged go‑live.

  7. 7) Training & Change Management

    Hands‑on training for handhelds, portals, SOPs, and exceptions. Typical training window: 1–2 days depending on team size/complexity.

  8. 8) Post‑Go‑Live Support & Scale‑Up

    24/7 remote support, scheduled on‑site visits, continuous monitoring, and preventive maintenance. Add BI & analytics to scale across sites.

Indicative timelines: small retail/office 1–2 weeks; larger warehouses 4–6 weeks; turnkey 2–6 weeks depending on scope.

Malaysia Compliance (At a Glance)

UHF RFID Band

Malaysia allocates 919–923 MHz for UHF RFID (refer MCMC SRSP‑530). Choose readers/tags that explicitly support the MY band.

Class Assignment

Use the latest MCMC Class Assignment (No. 2 of 2025) for licence‑exempt conditions, labeling, and device classes.

Type Approval / Imports

If importing readers/printers, confirm local approvals and any SIRIM procedures before deployment.

This page is informational; always verify current regulations with MCMC/SIRIM and your implementation partner.
